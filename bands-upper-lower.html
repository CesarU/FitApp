Training with bands

  • Chase the burn, not the number. Bands don't max out like dumbbells — lean into higher reps (12–20+) rather than trying to hit a fixed weight.
  • Slow the eccentric. A 3–4 second lowering phase keeps tension on the muscle since resistance drops near the start of each rep.
  • Keep 2–3 resistance levels on hand. Light, medium, heavy lets you match the band to the exercise, and to stack bands as you get stronger.
  • A door anchor changes everything. Rows, presses, face pulls, and pull-aparts all get easier to load well with one.
